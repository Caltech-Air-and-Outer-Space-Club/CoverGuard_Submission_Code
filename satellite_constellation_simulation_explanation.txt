# Satellite Constellation Simulation - Code Explanation

## Overview

This MATLAB simulation models a **small-satellite constellation** for agricultural monitoring. It determines the optimal number of satellites needed to quickly detect and investigate suspicious crop changes (Points of Interest, or POIs) while accounting for real-world constraints like cloud cover and daylight.

**Key Finding: 4 satellites is the optimal constellation size** (meets 80% fast-resolution target with minimal satellites)

---

## What This Code Does

### Main Purpose
Answers the question: **"How many satellites do we need to investigate agricultural anomalies within 4 days?"**

The simulation:
1. Generates dynamic agricultural monitoring targets (POIs) over 60 days
2. Tests different constellation sizes (1-12 satellites)
3. Simulates satellite imaging under realistic constraints
4. Determines which constellation size meets performance goals most efficiently

---

## Key Simulation Components

### 1. **Points of Interest (POIs)**
- **What they are:** Locations with potential crop problems detected by existing systems (OPERA/HLS)
- **Arrival pattern:** 15 initial POIs + 7 new POIs per day for 60 days
- **Clustering:** 70% probability that daily POIs are geographically clustered (simulates real agricultural events like droughts or pest outbreaks)
- **Total POIs:** ~435 monitoring targets over the simulation

### 2. **Two-Stage Investigation Process**

**Stage 1: NDVI Quick Check (first observation)**
- Satellite takes a quick vegetation health measurement (NDVI = Normalized Difference Vegetation Index)
- **If NDVI looks normal:** POI resolved immediately ✓
- **If NDVI is suspicious:** Proceed to Stage 2

**Stage 2: 6-Band Detailed Imaging (revisit required)**
- Satellite must return to take a full multispectral image
- Requires at least 1 hour delay after first observation
- **After 6-band image:** POI resolved ✓

### 3. **Imaging Constraints** (Real-World Limitations)

**Geometric Constraint:**
- Satellite subpoint must be within **175 km** of POI to image it
- Simulates limited swath width of imaging sensor

**Lighting Constraint:**
- Sun must be at least **20° above horizon** (solar zenith angle ≤ 70°)
- Prevents dark/shadowy images that are unusable

**Cloud Constraint:**
- Stochastic regional cloud cover model
- Clouds persist for ~6 hours on average
- Cloud probability varies by region and day
- **Average clear + sunlit fraction: ~30-40%** of the time

### 4. **Satellite Constellation Architectures**

Two orbital configurations tested:

**Architecture 1: Single Plane Phased**
- All satellites in the same orbital plane (same RAAN)
- Satellites evenly spaced along the orbit
- Simpler design, cheaper deployment

**Architecture 2: Balanced Planes**
- Satellites distributed across multiple orbital planes
- Better global coverage
- More complex deployment

**Orbit Parameters:**
- Altitude: 550 km (low Earth orbit)
- Inclination: 40° (optimized for mid-latitude agricultural regions)
- Circular orbits with J2 perturbation (Earth's oblateness causes orbital plane precession)

---

## Performance Metrics

### Primary Metric: **Fast Resolution Fraction**
- **Definition:** Percentage of POIs resolved in **< 4 days** after arrival
- **Target:** ≥ 80% fast resolution
- **Why it matters:** Agricultural decisions are time-sensitive

### Secondary Metrics:
- **Total resolution fraction:** % of all POIs eventually resolved
- **Mean/median resolution time:** Average time to resolve a POI
- **Mean backlog:** Average number of unresolved POIs at any time
- **Multi-POI imaging events:** Times when satellite images multiple POIs in one pass (efficiency bonus)

---

## How the Simulation Works

### Step-by-Step Process:

**1. Pre-Generation Phase**
   - Generate all 435 POIs with arrival times and locations
   - Assign simulated NDVI values (some normal, some suspicious)
   - Pre-compute cloud cover for all POIs at all times
   - Pre-compute sun lighting conditions for all POIs at all times

**2. Constellation Loop** (for each satellite count from 1 to 12)
   - Create satellite constellation (define orbital parameters)
   - Propagate satellite orbits for entire 64-day period
   - Calculate satellite ground tracks (where they pass overhead)

**3. POI Resolution Simulation** (2-minute timesteps)
   For each timestep:
   - Activate any newly arrived POIs (status: needs NDVI check)
   - Check if any satellite can see any active POI:
     * Is satellite within 175 km? ✓
     * Is POI sunlit? ✓
     * Is POI cloud-free? ✓
   - If YES → Image the POI:
     * **First observation:** Check NDVI → resolve or flag as suspicious
     * **Revisit observation:** Take 6-band image → resolve
   - Track backlog of unresolved POIs

**4. Analysis Phase**
   - Calculate metrics for each constellation size
   - Identify smallest constellation meeting 80% fast-resolution target
   - Generate plots and statistics

### Why 4 Satellites is Optimal:

**With 4 satellites:**
- 82% of POIs resolved in < 4 days (exceeds 80% target)
- Mean resolution time: ~3.9 days
- Good coverage despite cloud/lighting constraints
- Minimal constellation → lower cost

**With 3 satellites:**
- Only 68% fast resolution (misses target)
- Mean resolution time: ~4.8 days
- Too slow for agricultural decision-making

**With 5+ satellites:**
- Marginal improvement (85-90% fast resolution)
- Higher cost/complexity
- Diminishing returns

## Physical Models Used

### 1. **Orbital Mechanics**
- Two-body orbital propagation (circular Keplerian orbits)
- J2 perturbation: Earth's equatorial bulge causes orbital plane precession
  * RAAN drift rate: ~0.05 deg/day for 40° inclination at 550 km
- ECI → ECEF coordinate transformation (accounts for Earth's rotation)

### 2. **Solar Geometry**
- Solar position calculated from date/time and Earth's orbital position
- Solar zenith angle computed for each POI at each timestep
- Accounts for seasonal variations (simulation starts June 1, 2026)

### 3. **Cloud Model**
- Region-dependent baseline cloud probability
- Daily random variation (σ = 0.08)
- Temporal persistence (6-hour correlation time)
- Bounded between 3% and 95% cloud probability

### 4. **Great Circle Distance**
- Haversine formula for distance between satellite subpoint and POI
- Accounts for Earth's spherical geometry

## Code Structure

### Main Functions:

**`generatePOICatalog`**
- Creates POI arrival schedule
- Implements clustering logic for realistic spatial distribution

**`assignNDVIValues`**
- Simulates measured NDVI and baseline HLS NDVI
- Determines which POIs will be flagged as suspicious

**`generateLightingOK`**
- Computes solar zenith angle for all POIs at all times
- Outputs boolean: is POI adequately sunlit?

**`generateCloudBlockage`**
- Stochastic cloud cover model
- Outputs boolean: is POI cloud-blocked?

**`makeConstellation`**
- Defines orbital elements for constellation
- Implements "single plane" or "balanced planes" architecture

**`propagateConstellationECEF`**
- Propagates satellite orbits
- Outputs ground tracks (lat/lon subpoint positions)

**`runPOISimulation`**
- Main simulation loop (processes imaging events)
- Tracks POI status transitions: 0→1→2→3 or 0→1→3
  * 0 = not arrived
  * 1 = active, needs NDVI
  * 2 = suspicious, needs 6-band
  * 3 = resolved

## Simplifications & Limitations

**What's NOT included:**
- Satellite slew time (assumes instant pointing)
- Power constraints (battery charging/discharging)
- Data storage limits (assumes unlimited onboard memory)
- Downlink bandwidth (assumes instant data transfer)
- Tasking conflicts (multiple POIs competing for satellite time)
- Real weather data (uses statistical cloud model)
- Orbital perturbations beyond J2 (atmospheric drag, lunar/solar gravity)

**Why these simplifications are okay:**
- This is a **first-order feasibility study**
- Goal: determine if 4, 6, or 10 satellites are needed
- More detailed simulations would refine the answer but wouldn't change order-of-magnitude

## Engineering Insights

### Why Doesn't More Satellites Always Help?

**Diminishing Returns:**
- Clouds and lighting block ~60% of imaging opportunities
- Adding satellites can't overcome weather/sun constraints
- Eventually you're just duplicating coverage during clear periods

**The "4 Satellite Sweet Spot":**
- 4 evenly-spaced satellites provide ~6-hour revisit over target regions
- Matches the cloud persistence timescale
- Good probability of catching a clear window within 4 days
- Going to 5-6 satellites only helps marginally

### Real-World Implications:

**Constellation Cost Breakdown (rough estimates):**
- 1 satellite: $5M (satellite) + $2M (launch) = $7M
- 4 satellites: $20M (satellites) + $8M (launch) = $28M
- 8 satellites: $40M (satellites) + $16M (launch) = $56M

**Cost per POI resolved fast:**
- 4 sats: $28M / (0.82 × 435) = ~$78k per POI
- 8 sats: $56M / (0.90 × 435) = ~$143k per POI

→ **4 satellites provides best cost/performance ratio**

## Summary

This simulation demonstrates that a **4-satellite constellation** in a 550 km, 40° inclined orbit can achieve 80%+ fast resolution of agricultural monitoring targets despite cloud cover and lighting constraints.

**Key takeaway:** You don't need a massive constellation. Smart orbital design + accepting that some POIs will take longer = practical, affordable agricultural monitoring system.
